About Me

I am an R&D advisor, behavioural science practitioner, and data privacy consultant working at the intersection of research, policy, and applied technology. Through R&D Nordic, I support universities, public bodies, and innovation partners in designing, funding, and delivering complex projects involving AI, data, and cross-sector collaboration.

My work focuses on turning strategy into execution. This includes grant scanning and positioning, consortium building, budgeting, proposal development, and advisory support throughout project delivery. I have contributed to research and innovation programmes totalling approximately Â£50 million in awarded funding, including large multi-partner and international initiatives such as Horizon Europe.

A core part of my practice is translating regulation into workable systems. I advise on GDPR, research ethics, and emerging AI governance, helping organisations move from compliance on paper to operational readiness in practice. I am currently finalising an MSc in Behavioural Science, with a thesis examining organisational and business readiness for the EU AI Act, using an applied, behaviourally grounded approach.

My professional background includes early work as a healthcare professional in the NHS, which continues to shape how I approach communication, negotiation, and decision-making under pressure. I regularly deliver training and workshops on communication, stakeholder management, and behavioural aspects of change within research, innovation, and public-sector contexts.

I am also a member of the Digi Innlandet AI Expert Group, contributing to regional and national discussions on responsible AI adoption, capability building, and public-sector preparedness.

R&D Nordic exists to bridge policy and practice. I work best where complexity is high, incentives are misaligned, and clear thinking is required to move from ambition to delivery.
