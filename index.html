About Andrew Davidson

I am Andrew Davidson, an R&D advisor, behavioural science practitioner, and data privacy consultant working at the intersection of research, policy, and applied technology. Through R&D Nordic, I support universities, public bodies, and innovation partners in designing, funding, and delivering complex projects involving AI, data, and cross-sector collaboration.

I have supported more than 250 research and innovation applications across the Research Council of Norway, Horizon Europe, Erasmus+, NordForsk, Interreg, and related programmes. Across these applications, the total requested funding volume has been approximately NOK 500 million (GBP/EUR equivalents vary by exchange rate and year).

I have also contributed to 300+ governance and compliance deliverables, including DPAs, RoPAs, DPIAs, GDPR reviews, data-processing approvals, audit support, and contract-related privacy work.

My professional background includes early work as a healthcare professional in the NHS, which continues to shape how I approach communication, negotiation, and decision-making under pressure. I regularly deliver training and workshops on communication, stakeholder management, and behavioural aspects of change in research, innovation, and public-sector settings.

I am also a member of the Digi Innlandet AI Expert Group, contributing to regional and national discussions on responsible AI adoption and public-sector capability building.

LinkedIn: linkedin.com/in/rdnordic

R&D Nordic exists to bridge policy and practice. I work best where complexity is high, incentives are misaligned, and clear thinking is required to move from ambition to delivery.

Featured cases

Erasmus+ multi-country higher education capacity project

Context: Norwegian and Icelandic higher education institutions collaborating with three Sub-Saharan African partner institutions.

Role & Action: Led communication support, assisted project management, built GDPR and secure data-handling workflows, and supported research-data and IT-capacity implementation at partner institutions. Also coordinated partner validation resolution with the EU project officer in Brussels.

Outcome: Partner validation completed, compliant data collection/storage/handling in place, stronger PhD uptake and staff-student exchanges, and all core programme deliverables met.

Defence education AI capability programme (Norway)

Context: Ongoing training with Norwegian cyberdefence students and staff.

Role & Action: Delivered AI foundations, tailored RAG assistant design, and applied ethics, IPR, and GDPR training.

Outcome: 100+ staff and students trained to date, with continued collaboration and practical uptake in academic workflows.

Hospital AI enablement training (South-East & Western Norway)

Context: Multi-site capability building for clinical and administrative teams.

Role & Action: Delivered targeted training on AI adoption, tailored RAG assistants, and implementation guardrails for ethics, IPR, and GDPR.

Outcome: Cross-hospital training delivered with ongoing demand for continued upskilling and governance support.

Funding and governance support across research portfolios

Context: Multi-programme support across Norwegian and EU funding routes.

Role & Action: Supported 250+ applications and 300+ governance/compliance deliverables (DPA, RoPA, DPIA, GDPR reviews, approvals, and audit-oriented documentation).

Outcome: Improved submission quality, stronger operational readiness, and more consistent compliance foundations across projects.
