# Hi, I'm Andrew

R&D advisor at [R&D Nordic](https://rdnordic.com), based in Norway.

I work in the bits most people try to avoid: **privacy, research ethics, AI, and organisational behavioural management**. My job is helping organisations move from policy intent to practical, defensible implementation, without panic, paralysis, or magical thinking. I am equally parts fascinated and terrified by the pace of gen AI and have decided to ride this wave rather than be swept away. I have invested around 3500 hours into AI-systems training - Claude.ai / Claude Code, ChatGPT / Codex are my primary tools but I dabble in most of them. I teach this stuff at a Norwegian University, and am a core member of a few AI competency panels around the country with the aim of making sure we bring as many people along for the ride as we can.

There are people like me out there who care about this stuff, if you have holes in your systems that can affect the privacy rights of your customers, we will find it, and we can help you plug it.

## What I do

**GDPR & EU AI Act**: Translating rules into decisions, controls, and documentation that stand up when someone asks awkward questions.

**AI systems that actually work**: Retrieval-augmented systems for organisations using Copilot/UiO GPT/Sikt KI Chat, evaluation frameworks for private and public sector, automation pipelines in VS code (orchestrators / agent- sub agent workflows), with privacy, intellectual property rights, ethics, Record of processing activities, and risk management designed in from day one (not bolted on five minutes before the auditor, i.e. me, arrives).

**Grant development**: Partnership building, grant scanning, budgeting, and proposal work across the Norwegian Research Council, Horizon Europe, Erasmus+, and more.

**Training & adoption**: Project management, leadership, communication, and how behavioural science and Organisational Behavioural Management can make adoption real rather than turning governance into theatre.

## Background

Higher education, health, public administration, and the cultural and creative sectors mainly in Norway and the UK but open to collab internationally. I'm especially interested in how we get people to actually change what they do, not just nod along in the workshop and go back to business as usual.
